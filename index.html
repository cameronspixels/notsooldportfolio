Cameron Barnes

I'm a final year Web Development student at Manchester Metropolitan University.

Let's take a look at some things I've done.

Digital Space

I've been in programming on and off since I was around 15. Creating mods and running game servers for the early, indie versions of the Minecraft was really just a hobby, but one that I got really involved in. I learnt a lot about Java as a programming language, and how servers work from a command line level. Strangely, it went quite far; you can find out more in this blog post.

I originally came to Manchester studying Digital Media/Marketing, and continued to do so for 2 years. Realising that I was more in digital, I made the decision at the end of my penultimate year to focus on the web. As such, I entered my final year taking the opportunity to switch my degree course to Web Development.

Business Spirit

Switching my course doens't mean de-valuing what I learnt in marketing.

I've noticed that every time I run through ideas, alone or as a group, I jump to whether it would work as a model within business, or whether or not it's marketable.

Though it doesn't pertain specifically to the role I'd like to graduate into, I still appreciate it. It stops me jumpig to the first idea that pops into my head, or looks nice on paper. In that respect, what I've learnt over 2 years all feels relevant in both design and development.
